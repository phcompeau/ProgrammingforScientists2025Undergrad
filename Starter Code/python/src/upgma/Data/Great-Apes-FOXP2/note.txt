We retrieved FOXP2 (forkhead box protein P2) sequences for 9 species from UniProt/NCBI.

FOXP2 is famous as a gene implicated in human speech and language. Mutations cause severe speech 
and language impairment. Remarkably, the human FOXP2 protein differs from other great apes by only 
2 amino acids — a tiny but apparently crucial difference thought to have been under intense positive 
selection in the human lineage. The zebrafinch (Taeniopygia guttata) is included because FOXP2 is 
also essential for vocal learning in songbirds — young zebra finches with disrupted FOXP2 cannot 
properly learn their father's song. This parallel role in vocal learning in both humans and 
songbirds makes FOXP2 one of the most fascinating genes in evolutionary biology.
