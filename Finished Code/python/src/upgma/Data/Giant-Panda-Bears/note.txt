We retrieved cytochrome b protein sequences for 10 species in and around family Ursidae from NCBI.

The placement of the giant panda (Ailuropoda melanoleuca) was one of the most debated questions 
in mammalian systematics. For decades, biologists argued whether the giant panda was a bear 
(family Ursidae), a relative of the red panda (family Ailuridae), or something else entirely. 
Molecular data definitively placed the giant panda within Ursidae — and the red panda in its 
own distinct family. The spectacled bear (Tremarctos ornatus) serves as the most basal true bear.

Pairwise distances are Levenshtein (edit) distances between cytochrome b protein sequences.
