We retrieved mitochondrial cytochrome b protein sequences for 12 mammalian species from NCBI.

This dataset illustrates one of the most surprising discoveries of molecular phylogenetics: 
whales and dolphins (order Cetacea) are not merely related to even-toed ungulates (order 
Artiodactyla) — they evolved from within Artiodactyla. In fact, the hippopotamus is more 
closely related to whales than it is to other hoofed mammals like horses or rhinos. This 
finding was deeply counterintuitive based on morphology alone, and required molecular data 
to establish. The horse, rhinoceros, and elephant serve as outgroup comparisons.

Pairwise distances are Levenshtein (edit) distances between cytochrome b protein sequences.
